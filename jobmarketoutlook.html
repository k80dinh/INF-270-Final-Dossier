Job-Market Outlook

With technology continuing to advance and more GLAM institutions move towards digital access, the demand for LIS professionals with technological expertise is actually expected to increase (although at a slower rate than the average occupation). According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the amount of roles for Library Media Specialists will rise 2% by 2034—with around 13,500 positions expected to open due to retirement (UNLV, 2025). Such a slow projection is consistent with Heather's observation that jobs within her field are rare. The roles that do exist are shifting towards data management, digital preservation, user-experience, and digital asset management.

With this in mind, the jobs within the field are not exclusive to MLIS degree holders. A range of applicable skills, including IT work, web-design, and computation, can lead to a technology-based role, particularly when it comes to entry-level positions.

To advance towards a position like Heather’s, there is no straightforward path. Instead, various entry points exist, like IT roles, technical assistant positions, and more. Experience is key. Leadership, organization, management, problem solving, cultural heritage preservation, and creative skills are critical for a technological leadership role in a GLAM institution.
