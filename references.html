Annotated Bibliography

  • “Digital and Online Services in Libraries,” American Library Association, accessed March 11, 2026,https://www.ala.org/support/digital-and-online-services-libraries.

    This source from the American Library Association is a broad list of relevant, informative, and educational materials on operating and creating digital and online services in libraries. Furthermore, it also serves as a bibliography for relevant literature on digital librarianship.

  • “Librarians and Media Collections Specialists,” O*NET OnLine, updated 2026, https://www.onetonline.org/link/summary/25-4022.00.

    O*NET OnLine is sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor, and provides statistics and information on a range of careers, including library and information science roles. This is an informative resource, with guides that include work tasks, technology skills, work activities and contexts, required experiences

  • “Librarians and Media Collections Specialists Occupational Requirements Survey,” Occupational Outlook Handbook, Bureau of Labor Statistics, modified August 28, 2025,https://www.bls.gov/ooh/education-training-and-library/librarians.htm.

    This source from the U.S. Department of Labor shares data on library and library media specialist roles in 2024, as well as predictions for the future of the career. It includes pay, job market outlook statistics, work environment details, and other relevant information.

  • “Library Professionals: Facts & Figures,” Department for Professional Employees, accessed March 11, 2026, https://www.dpeaflcio.org/factsheets/library-professionals-facts-and-figures.

    This source shares a very recent (2025) fact sheet on library profession data, including demographics of professionals, wages, health benefits, and union information.

  • "u/Phoenicianredware, “Museum pros with technology-focused jobs,” Reddit, posted 2020, https://www.reddit.com/r/MuseumPros/comments/ht2rht/museum_pros_with_technologyfocused_jobs/.

    This is a useful, relatively recent Reddit thread focused on technology jobs in museums. Users shared how they found these roles, and their skills, backgrounds, and responsibilities, as well as how they see the job market changing.
