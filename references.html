Annotated Bibliography

  • “Digital and Online Services in Libraries,” American Library Association, accessed March 11, 2026,https://www.ala.org/support/digital-and-online-services-libraries.

    This source from the American Library Association is a broad list of relevant, informative, and educational materials on operating and creating digital and online services in libraries. Furthermore, it also serves as a bibliography for relevant literature on digital librarianship. Read about how to create and manage electronic collections, as well as current issues and practices.

  • Parry, Ross, Vince Dziekan, and Karin de Wild. 2025. Museums and Digital Confidence: Organisation, Collection, Interface. 1st ed. Edited by Ross Parry, Karin de Wild, and Vince Dziekan. Routledge. https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003269601.

    This book provides an excellent overview about the evolution of digital practices within museums. For students and aspiring practitioners, the book also details the necessary skills and values needed for technology to be successfully integrated within an institution. By compiling works by IS professionals, museologists, and curators, the volume has more information on topics directly related to Heather’s role, such as: digital curation and the successful internal organization of digital teams. Read if you would like a management perspective on technologies in museums.

  • McKee, Margaret C, and Jessica Herczeg-Konecny. 2025. Digital Asset Management for Museums. 1st ed. Bloomsbury Publishing. https://doi.org/10.5040/9798881844226.

    A large number of job descriptions that fall under the umbrella of digital work in GLAM often require knowledge of Digital Asset Management. This book is a comprehensive overview for any student looking to learn more about DAMS and its capabilities within a museum setting—from improving information retrieval, facilitating public access, and minimizing data loss.

  • “Library Professionals: Facts & Figures,” Department for Professional Employees, accessed March 11, 2026, https://www.dpeaflcio.org/factsheets/library-professionals-facts-and-figures.

    This source shares a very recent (2025) fact sheet on library profession data (including digital librarianship). Students can read about the demographics of professionals, wages, health benefits, and union information. All information is modeled into helpful and easily-digestible charts!

  • "u/Phoenicianredware, “Museum pros with technology-focused jobs,” Reddit, posted 2020, https://www.reddit.com/r/MuseumPros/comments/ht2rht/museum_pros_with_technologyfocused_jobs/.

    This is a useful, relatively recent Reddit thread focused on technology jobs in museums. Users shared how they found these roles, and their skills, backgrounds, and responsibilities, as well as how they see the job market changing. A great way to see what working professionals are actually doing!
