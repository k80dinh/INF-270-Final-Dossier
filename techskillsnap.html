Technical Skill Snapshot

At the entry level, this specialization begins with a robust understanding of the software, data, and systems necessary to support a library, museum, or archive.

We do predict a growing demand for skills in software and programming. However, this may change as AI and technology continues to advance. Heather advises that technology changes quickly, and not to overemphasize these skills. Familiarity is more important than mastery. Internet and technology literacy will continue to be crucial in this specialization, as well as hands-on capabilities when it comes to managing systems. Like Heather shared with us, hands-on technical work is more common for entry-level roles in this specialization.

Furthermore, technology skills will remain critical as born-online collections continue to grow and require collection management. Because of the need to digitally preserve cultural heritage materials, as well as make collections available for online access, the need for those with skills in data organization, software, and design may grow. Likewise, cultural competency and communication skills are also critical in the field, especially as a leader or manager. These higher-level roles, like Heather’s, combine technical skills, leadership experience, problem-solving capabilities, and experience with cultural heritage institutions.
