Technical Skill Snapshot

At the entry level, this specialization in digital and technological work begins with a robust understanding of software, data, and systems necessary to support a library, museum, or archive. We do predict a growing demand for skills in software and programming, however, these may change form and shape as AI and technology continues to advance. Heather advises that software changes quickly, and not to overemphasize these skills. Internet and technology literacy will continue to be important in this specialization, as well as hands-on capabilities when it comes to managing systems. Like Heather shared with us, hands-on technical work in the institutional realm is a regular task for entry-level roles in this specialization. Furthermore, technology skills will remain critical as born-online collections continue to grow and require collection management.

Because of the need to digitally preserve cultural heritage materials, as well as make collections available for online access, the need for those with skills in data organization, software, and design may grow. Likewise, cultural competency and communication skills are also critical for cultural preservation work, especially as a leader or manager. These higher-level roles, like Heather’s, exist at the intersection of technical skills, leadership experience, problem-solving capabilities, and experience with cultural heritage institutions, or similar spaces.
