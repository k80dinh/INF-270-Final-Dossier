Training Agenda

6) Training agenda: In order to enter this profession, what kinds of preparation should someone at your professional level undertake? Are there particular classes they should take? Should they be looking for particular kinds of internships? Should they target particular technical skills?

Senior level positions for Digital and Technology Management require a diverse blend of hard and soft skills that can be utilized across various different software systems used in information technology rather than relying on one specific programming language. This can make pathways into Information Science less straightforward than some STEM careers. Heather herself, for example, was a print journalism and political science major. Rather than targeting a particular degree or set of classes, gaining a broader understanding of the thought process behind integrating digital systems into a single organizational plan is ideal preparation for this field.

Institutional Systems and Technologies

Organizational knowledge and prioritization over perfection are key to working in this field. Institutions may manage dozens or even hundreds of file databases requiring preservation planning and coordination. Rather than expecting employees to know every system, the goal is to structure information systems so that teams can collaborate effectively across departments.

  • Large institutional databases requiring preservation planning
  • Shared CRM system for guest and ticketing management
  • Sierra ILS – library and archival collections
  • ContentDM – digital library repository
  • AM – circulation system for cards
  • TMS – art museum collections database
  • BGBase – botanical garden database
  • FGIS mapping software
