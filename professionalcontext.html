Heather did not share any professional organizations that she has membership in, but we recommend looking into the Visual Resources Association. They co-host the Summer Education Institute along with ARLIS—providing workshops about digital practices and curation within GLAM institutions. Individuals can learn about DAMS, digital preservation, ethical digital stewardship, and collections management.

But with the increasing importance of digital repositories, collections, and artistic work, there is a growing network of professionals that focus on bridging archive and collection creation with DAMS. The Huntington itself has made strides in digitizing their various collections that can be made accessible to the public, including manuscripts, maps, and photographs. They’ve also made efforts to educate the public on how to utilize these digital resources for archival self-management or other institutional purposes in their Collections up Close: The Digital Collection as a Tool for Close Examination.
